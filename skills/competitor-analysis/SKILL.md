# Competitor Analysis

## Purpose

Analyze competitor moves with evidence and translate them into PM decisions.

## Use Cases

- Competitor launches a new feature.
- Team considers copying or counter-positioning.
- PM needs a market scan for strategy review.

## Required Inputs

- Competitor name.
- Feature or move to analyze.
- Source links or screenshots.
- Target user and market context.
- Your product's strategic goal.

## Procedure

1. Capture what changed with source links and dates.
2. Separate observable facts from interpretation.
3. Identify target segment, user job, and business intent.
4. Compare with your product's context and constraints.
5. Decide whether to ignore, monitor, test, respond, or lead differently.
6. Propose a small validation if action is needed.

## Output Contract

```markdown
## What Changed
## Evidence
## User Job
## Strategic Intent
## Relevance To Us
## Recommended Response
## Validation Plan
## Watch Items
```

## Quality Checks

- Every factual claim has a source or is marked unverified.
- Do not assume competitor success from launch alone.
- Explain why the move matters or does not matter.

## Human Gates

PM must decide:

- Whether to respond.
- Whether to change roadmap.
- Whether to communicate externally.

## Example

Recommendation: "Monitor, do not copy. The competitor feature targets high-frequency commuters; our current opportunity is family weekend planning."

## Common Failure Modes

- Copying features without understanding business context.
- Treating PR language as product evidence.
- Ignoring your own product constraints.
